### Dinner Traditions

The dinner and its traditions is probably the part of the wedding that is most different in spanish eyes. You thought the dinner was simply about... eating your dinner? Absolutely not. Here you can read what to expect will happen while you trie to savour your meal. Note, that danish traditions are not just rules that one must observe. Some of our traditions are interactive, where the guests play a key role, while others embue them with power over the couple.

#### Kissing the brid or groom when one leaves the room





During the dinner the bride and groom are seated together. If one of them leaves to go to the bathroom, is the window for the oppsite sex to hurry up and give the remaining party a kiss on the cheek - i.e. if the groom leaves, all the guys kiss the bride, and if the bride leaves, all the girls kiss the groom.

#### On demand kisses

At the whim of the guests, when they hit their glasses or plate with their cuttlery, the groom and bride have to get up onto their chairs and kiss. Likewise, when the guests stamp their feet, the groom and bride have to get under their table and kiss. This can occur at any point during the dinner, as many times in a row, as the guests see fit.
